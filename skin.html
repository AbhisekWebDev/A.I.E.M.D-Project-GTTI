Everything You need to Know about skin disease


What is a skin disease?
Skin diseases are conditions that affect your skin. These diseases may cause rashes, inflammation, itchiness or other skin changes. Some skin conditions may be genetic, while lifestyle factors may cause others. Skin disease treatment may include medications, creams or ointments, or lifestyle changes. Skin cancer is one of the most serious conditions dermatologists treat, and while most forms of skin cancer are slow progressing, the sooner the treatment begins, the better the patient's prognosis will be.

Why skin disease happens?
Common causes of skin diseases include: Bacteria trapped in your pores or hair follicles. Conditions that affect your thyroid, kidneys or immune system. Contact with environmental triggers, such as allergens or another person's skin. Allergies, irritants, genetic makeup, certain diseases, and immune system problems can cause skin conditions. Stress can also: Make skin problems worse. For example, stress can aggravate psoriasis, rosacea, and eczema. It can also cause hives and other types of skin rashes and trigger a flare-up of fever blisters.

What are the symptoms of skin diseases?
Discolored skin patches (abnormal pigmentation).
Dry skin.
Open sores, lesions or ulcers.
Peeling skin.
Rashes, possibly with itchiness or pain.
Red, white or pus-filled bumps.
Scaly or rough skin.


How are skin diseases treated?
Wash your hands often and properly. Wash scrapes, cuts, wounds, or insect bites right away. Do not share items, such as towels, razors, bar soap, clothes, or toys. Avoid skin-to-skin contact with anyone who has a skin infection.
Chronic skin conditions typically aren't curable, but they can be managed using drugs and by paying close attention to your lifestyle. Learn more about symptoms, treatments, and ways to feel better.
